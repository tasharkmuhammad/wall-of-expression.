Mateo Arriaga

Primary Work: “Intertwined” (Clasped Hands)

Second Work: The Two Men Embracing

Background Story:
Mateo Arriaga is a community-based muralist whose work explores solidarity across racial and cultural lines. Raised between two cultures, Mateo grew up navigating identity in layered spaces. His art focuses on connection as resistance — the simple, radical act of choosing to hold on to one another.

Zion Walker

Primary Work: “Color Carried Forward” (Rainbow Flag)

Second Work: Rainbow Over Water

Background Story:
Zion Walker is a visual storyteller focused on joy within the LGBTQ+ community. Their work centers visibility, pride, and the celebration of identity without apology.

Eliana Cruz

Primary Work: “Rooted Here” (Heart with Mountains)

Second Work: Salt Lake City Skyline

Background Story:
Eliana Cruz creates work that ties identity to place. Growing up near mountain ranges, she developed a deep belief that landscapes carry memory.

Daniel Kim

Second Work: Flowers in Bloom

Background Story:
Daniel Kim’s work centers collective joy and intergenerational celebration. Inspired by community festivals and neighborhood gatherings, his pieces emphasize movement, color, and shared momentum.
