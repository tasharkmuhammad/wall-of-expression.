Not a mural — a living wall.

The Wall of Expression is an evolving public surface where community voices can appear, layer, and change over time. It’s designed as a shared place for reflection, storytelling, and visibility — not a single finished image.

What it is

An evolving wall for messages, responses, and shared memory.

How people engage

Move along the wall, pause on benches, read, reflect, and return.

Why it matters

Creates space for public voice and belonging in a changing city.

Concept + experience

The wall is meant to feel open and adaptable. Instead of presenting one official story, it invites many voices to coexist. Over time, the wall becomes a record of community presence — layered, revisited, and shaped by the people who use it.

  • Messages can layer rather than replace.
  • Multiple perspectives can exist at the same time.
  • People can return and notice what has changed.
  • Benches support slower viewing, reflection, and conversation.
